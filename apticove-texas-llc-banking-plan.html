◆ The decision, with eyes open

Texas it is - here are the two guardrails that make it safe

You chose Texas over Wyoming. Good news: your plan for possible US offices later makes Texas more defensible than a pure-remote setup would be. Two things need to be done right, and this plan bakes both in.

Guardrail 1 · Privacy

Form it manager-managed

Texas normally publishes the owners' names. Filing manager-managed (optionally with a holding entity as the manager) keeps both of your names off every public Texas record. Full playbook in section 2.

Guardrail 2 · The May 15 report

Never miss the annual filing

Miss the Public Information Report and Texas can strip your liability shield and make you personally liable. Fix: a registered agent (or CPA) files it for you every May 15, automatically. Section 5.

2 Keep your names private

The privacy structure

Texas exposes owners through two documents: the Certificate (public forever) and the yearly Public Information Report. Here is how to show as little as possible.

With a holding entity as the manager, neither brother's name touches any public Texas filing.

Simple route

One brother as sole manager

  • Exactly one name on record, with a business mailing address (never a home address)
  • Zero extra entities or cost
  • Ownership (the 50/50) still stays private
Max-privacy route

A holding entity as manager

  • Zero brother names on any Texas public record
  • Adds ~$100-$200/yr for the second entity + a slightly more complex tax picture CPA
  • Clean, well-established structure
The registered agent alone does not hide you. It shields the office address and receives legal mail, but the manager line is separate. Agent privacy + manager-managed structure work together, not instead of each other.
3 Agent & the partnership contract

Registered agent + Company Agreement

Registered agent · required, ~$125/yr

A Texas street address that receives legal mail. You cannot self-serve from Pakistan.

  • Northwest Registered Agent is the go-to for non-residents (privacy-forward, can act as organizer)
  • Non-negotiable: pick one that also files your annual PIR (or guarantees reminders). This is what defuses the May 15 forfeiture risk.
Company Agreement · private, not filed

Texas's name for the operating agreement. It holds the ownership record you keep OFF public filings, and it is what protects two brothers from a 50/50 stalemate.

Draft 80% from a solid manager-managed template, then attorney review the exit + deadlock clauses before signing.

The clauses that matter for 50/50 brothers

  • Ownership + capital: 50/50, each capital account
  • Distributions: split + timing, consent to draws
  • Decision rights: what needs both signatures (debt, hiring, selling, new members) vs day-to-day
  • Future funding: what if one cannot fund a call (dilution)
  • Right of first refusal: neither sells to an outsider first
  • Buyout: valuation method + payment terms + triggers
  • Deadlock (essential): a shotgun clause (one names a price, the other buys or sells at it) or binding arbitration
  • Dissolution + governing law: Texas
Do not sign a generic single-member template. A 50/50 split with no tiebreaker is the classic way two partners end up in court. The deadlock + buyout sections are the few hundred dollars of attorney review most worth spending.
4 Tax IDs

EIN now, ITIN later

The EIN unblocks all banking. The ITINs are a next-tax-season item; do not let them hold anything up.

Fastest - phone: call +1 267-941-1099 (Mon-Fri, 6am-11pm ET). Atif answers the SS-4 questions live and gets the EIN on the call. Have the form filled in front of you.
Or fax: send SS-4 to 304-707-9471 with a return fax number; EIN comes back in ~4 business days. The IRS online tool will reject you (needs a US SSN), so do not use it.
ITIN (Form W-7) · each brother · later

Individual tax IDs, needed when your first partnership return issues K-1s. Filed through a Certifying Acceptance Agent (so you never mail your real passport), normally attached to the first tax return. A Q1-next-year item your CPA handles. Turnaround 7-11 weeks. It does not block formation or banking.

5 The one date that matters

Franchise tax, the May 15 report & federal filing

Unmissable

Public Information Report - due every May 15

$0

Franchise tax owed below $2.65M revenue (you, easily)

But you STILL file

The PIR (Form 05-102) is required yearly even at $0 tax

Miss it →

$50 penalty, then forfeiture, then personal liability

First report due May 15, 2027 (the year after you form). Defuse it structurally: have your registered agent or CPA file the PIR every year, and set shared May 1 + May 15 reminders as backup. The task is tiny; the downside of forgetting is catastrophic.

Federal · route to a cross-border CPA

A 2-member LLC is a partnership federally: Form 1065 + a K-1 per brother + Schedules K-2/K-3 (mandatory with foreign partners), due March 15.

The favorable position: because all work is performed in Pakistan with no US office, employees, or agent, your income is very likely foreign-sourced and not "effectively connected" - meaning likely no US federal income tax and no withholding. It is a strong position, but get the CPA's written opinion so any future bank/Stripe review or audit has documentation. CPA in writing
6 The money rails

Banking & payments that actually work from Pakistan

The founder-favorite neobanks are closed to Pakistan. This is the stack that works, in the order to open it.

Open 1st · primary US rail

Wise Business

Real US routing + account number (the account everything else pays into) + multi-currency. ~$31 once, no monthly.

Watch: enhanced KYC for Pakistan - have website + a real invoice ready before applying, or it gets declined.

Open 2nd · backup rail

Airwallex

A second set of US details + FX. Your safety net so you are never single-threaded if Wise ever flags a Stripe payout.

Eligibility is based on where the entity is registered (US LLC qualifies).

Open 3rd · card acceptance

Stripe

Client cards + invoices. Needs the Wise/Airwallex account to exist first (that is the payout destination). 2.9% + $0.30 per charge.

Note: a plain Stripe account works even though Stripe Atlas does not serve Pakistan.

Open 4th · marketplaces

Payoneer

For marketplace inflows (Upwork/Amazon) and clean PKR withdrawals to your local bank (~$3 + ~2% FX).

Not a Stripe payout destination - keep it for what it does well.

PayPal Business - only on demand. Highest freeze risk of the stack. Open it only when a client insists, complete the W-8BEN-E (Certificate of Foreign Status) promptly, and sweep balances out fast rather than parking money there.
Do not waste time on these: Mercury, Relay, Brex, Rho. Mercury and Relay ban Pakistan-resident owners at the country level (a permanent decline, not a paperwork issue); Brex/Rho target funded US startups. Skip all four.

Also get: a US business address (virtual mailbox)

Stripe wants one and it makes KYC cleaner across providers. iPostal1 (~$10/mo) or Anytime Mailbox (~$10/mo) both onboard non-residents with a passport. Budget ~$120-$360/yr including scans.

7 Getting paid, at home

How the money reaches Pakistan

Client pays the US company, you convert and send it home the cheapest way, and you keep the Pakistani tax rate tiny by registering right.

*with PSEB registration - see below. Push clients toward direct ACH/bank transfer into Wise to pay near-zero processing.

Keep the Pakistan tax tiny - register with PSEB. Both brothers registering with the Pakistan Software Export Board (pseb.org.pk, ~Rs 1,000) drops the tax on inbound export remittances to 0.25% (vs 1% unregistered), and Section 65F offers up to 100% income-tax exemption on IT-export income through 2029 - but only if you receive through normal banking channels and still file annual returns. Confirm the LLC-profit-vs-personal-income treatment with a cross-border CPA + local advisor.
