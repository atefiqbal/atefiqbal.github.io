Homepage - the Frankenstack (common enemy)

You don't have a growth problem. You have a Frankenstack problem. The Amazon guy. The Meta girl. An SEO retainer that reports traffic nobody can tie to revenue. An email freelancer who exports a CSV once a month. A creative shop that ships assets no one briefed against the data. Five vendors. Five logins. Five invoices. And exactly one person connecting them: you. Do the math on the leak, not the retainers. A disconnected channel mix at $2M quietly burns 15 to 30 percent of paid efficiency to duplication, missed handoffs, and stale-data decisions. Call it $4,000 to $9,000 a month walking out the door while everyone hits their individual KPI. That's not a team. That's a stack of contractors stapled together, and you're the staple. The only teams that run all of this as one system are the enterprise agencies, and they gatekeep the fix behind $15,000 a month. Until now.

The flagship pillar (drafted, ~1,160 words, publish-ready)

Why Your Ecommerce Brand Is Invisible in ChatGPT (And How to Fix It)

Ask ChatGPT "best magnesium supplement for sleep" or "which standing desk is worth it under $500," and it will name five brands. If yours is not one of them, you are not losing a ranking. You are losing the recommendation, before a human ever sees a search results page. Why AI answer engines skip your brand: ChatGPT assembles answers from what others have written about your category. You fail on three counts: you're absent from the corroborating sources; your pages state no liftable claim ("premium quality" gives a model nothing to quote, "third-party tested, 400mg, ships from Ohio in 2 days" gives it a sentence); and you're optimized for a click, not a citation. How to get cited: (1) Seed the sources the model already trusts, get into the roundups, review platforms, and forum threads it retrieves from. This is digital PR reframed for machines. (2) Publish answer-shaped content, lead with the direct answer, question-shaped H2s, plain specifics, say who it's NOT for (reads as trustworthy). (3) Make specifics machine-legible with Product/FAQ/Organization schema and a clean entity footprint. (4) Measure the answer, run your 10 buying prompts monthly across ChatGPT/Perplexity/AI Mode, log who gets named and cited. Why it's a 2026 problem, not 2027: the brands compounding citations now, while the category is cheap to enter, get written into the answer their buyers already trust. That's the first thing we pull apart in a teardown. Where growth finds harbor.

Carries FAQ schema for 7 questions (How do I get recommended by ChatGPT / Why doesn't ChatGPT mention my product / What is AEO / etc.) + Article + BreadcrumbList schema. The real citation lever is off-page corroboration, so seed third-party roundups in parallel.

Creative strategy + 6 ad concepts

Buyer read: Problem Aware leaking into Solution Aware. The under-served emotion: exhaustion + self-inflicted shame ("I am the bottleneck, and I built it myself"). TOF job: name the constraint sharper than they can, then reveal the system fix. Offer belongs in retargeting, not cold.

Three non-negotiables: proof by craft not claim of craft (the ad is the sample); diagnostic before prescriptive in the visual too; AI in the mechanism, never the headline. North star: cost per booked teardown, not reach. All prompts use the official Harbor palette (Harbor Deep #0B3D3A, Harbor Gold #E3B23C, Cove Teal #12655C).
