D · Tax - what "Wyoming tax-free" really means

"No state income tax" is real. "No taxes at all" is a myth that gets founders penalized. Here's the precise line:

What you PAY & FILE
  • WY annual report: $60/yr + registered agent (~$125/yr)
  • Federal Form 1065 partnership return + two K-1s + K-2/K-3 (mandatory with foreign partners), due March 16 (extendable to Sep 15)
  • EIN (free) + an ITIN for each brother (Form W-7, via a Certifying Acceptance Agent)
  • Cross-border CPA: ~$800-2,000/yr (higher year one for EIN/ITIN + written ECI opinion)
What you likely DON'T owe (CPA confirms)
  • ✅ No WY state income tax, no franchise tax
  • ✅ Likely no US federal income tax - you both work physically in Pakistan, so the income is foreign-sourced and not "effectively connected." US clients + Stripe don't change that.
  • ✅ Likely no Section 1446 withholding (no 8804/8805)
  • ✅ No WY sales tax (B2B services exempt) · ✅ No BOI/FinCEN filing (US-formed LLCs exempt since Mar 2025)
The trap that bites: "Wyoming = no taxes" makes founders skip the Form 1065 filing. The late penalties are per-partner, per-month, even when zero tax is due. You file the 1065 + K-2/K-3 regardless. Engage the CPA before year-end, not at filing crunch - get the "no US income tax" (ECI) position in writing, and avoid ever creating US nexus (no US employee, office, agent, or in-US work days). (Pakistani-side tax on your income is a separate regime - a local advisor handles that.)
